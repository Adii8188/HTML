Iron Man

google me Iron Man is a superhero appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. Co-created by writer and editor Stan Lee, developed by scripter Larry Lieber, and designed by artists Don Heck and Jack Kirby, the character first appeared in Tales of Suspense #39 in 1963, and received his own title with Iron Man #1 in 1968. Shortly after his creation, Iron Man was a founding member of a superhero team, the Avengers

publication history

premiere

Following the success of the Fantastic Four in 1961 and the subsequent revival of American comic books featuring superheroes, Marvel Comics set about creating new superhero characters. Stan Lee developed the initial concept for Iron Man.

solo series

Iron Man was more overtly political than other Silver Age Marvel characters.[122] Lee wrote the character to represent liberal capitalism, fighting against communism and other anti-democratic forces.[123][124][125] Though anti-communist sentiments were present throughout Marvel Comics, they appeared most prominently in Iron Man stories.

Functional Character biography

As a child, Tony Stark inherits his family's business, Stark Industries, when his parents are killed in a car crash.[82] Developing equipment for the U.S. military, he travels to a war zone to conduct a weapons test when he triggers a booby trap. His heart is critically injured by shrapnel, and he is captured by the communist Wong-Chu, who demands Stark build him a weapon.
